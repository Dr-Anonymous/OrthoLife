    Understanding Gout and Diet

    Gout occurs when uric acid crystals build up in the joints, often causing sudden, severe pain, swelling, and redness. Since uric acid is formed when the body breaks down purines (found in certain foods), diet plays a crucial role in controlling gout.

    Foods to Limit or Avoid

    1. Red Meat and Organ Meats

    • Lamb, beef, pork, liver, kidney, and other organ meats are high in purines.

    2. Seafood

    • Anchovies, sardines, shellfish, tuna, and mackerel can raise uric acid levels.

    3. Tamarind & spicy gravies

    • Excess can worsen inflammation.

    4. Alcohol

    • Beer and spirits especially increase the risk of gout attacks.

    • Limit wine consumption.

    5. Sugary Drinks and Sweets

    • Soft drinks with high-fructose corn syrup and sweets can trigger gout flares.

    Foods That Help Manage Gout

    1. Low-Fat Dairy Products

    • Milk, yogurt, and cheese can lower uric acid levels.

    2. Vegetables

    • Leafy greens, ridge gourd, bottle gourd, beans, pumpkin, drumstick.

    3. Fruits

    • Cherries, strawberries, oranges, and other vitamin C‚Äìrich fruits help reduce uric acid.

    4. Whole Grains

    • Brown rice, oats, barley, and whole wheat bread are good choices.

    • Idli, dosa (with controlled dal proportion)

    5. Hydration

    • Drink 2‚Äì3 liters of water daily to flush out uric acid.

    Lifestyle Tips Along with Diet

    • Maintain a healthy body weight.

    • Exercise regularly but avoid overexertion during flare-ups.

    • Take medications as prescribed by your doctor.

    • Keep a food diary to track triggers.

    Conclusion

    A healthy, balanced diet is one of the best ways to prevent gout attacks and protect your joints. By limiting purine-rich foods and focusing on fruits, vegetables, whole grain, and hydration, you can significantly reduce flare-ups and live more comfortably.
